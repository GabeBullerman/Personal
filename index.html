Projects

Mobile App

MusiQuest is an android application created using Java consisting of multiple activity pages, and several operations communicating with the server and database which was created by my team and I. MusiQuest is an application intended for use of those teaching, or learning music theory in the form of a quiz-style game. MusiQuest has multiple WebSocket features such as a global chat, friends list, and a global leaderboard per level.

Web App

Lusiant is an ecommerce webapp created using JavaScript, HTML and CSS. This site is a user-friendly, interactive store for a clothing brand created by a friend of mine. This webapp is powered by a MongoDB database, and React for the frontend. Lusiant utilizes both tailwind and bootstrap CSS libraries for icons, and the building blocks of the catalog section of the site. This site also feature an administrator lock to shut down the site between productions of Lusiant products. Lusiant also contains a contact and payment form that uses autofill to streamline interactive forms throughout the site.

More on Me

Hobbies

I've always enjoyed practicing my spontaneity. I tend to pick up new hobbies that those around me don't expect. My most recent endeavors include, but are not limited to, piano, automotive mechanical work, and film. In my childhood home, there was a grand piano that nobody knew how to play. After moving out to college, I finally decided to honor the instrument that hadn't been played by anyone for years and have been self-teaching music theory and piano for the last few years. To keep people guessing as to what kind of person I was, I bought a Kawasaki sports bike. Once I bought it, I saw an infinite span of opportunity for learning. Within the last year, I have taken apart, rebuilt, and heavily modified said sports bike, and I loved every second of that learning and execution process. I've also dedicated an extraordinary amount of time to studying the art of film and watching the IMDB top 250 movies (Interstellar being my favorite).

Industry

Ever since I received my first, brand new, hand-me-down laptop at the age of 8, I have always had an infatuation with computers. Since then, between side work and helping friends and family, I have built over 40 desktop computers of all kinds. I remember distinctly a conversation about computers I had in high school with one of my childhood best friends, in which I was asked, "So you can build them and make them do stuff?" Though this question seems very surface-level and, frankly, silly. It sparked something inside of me. Since that moment, I have put computer hardware on the backburner and dedicated all of my time and passion for computers directly to software, making computers "do stuff". Sure, I could build a computer. But that computer depended on other people's creations to be valuable. I want to make computers valuable in that same way. I want to innovate and create new software that can be appreciated by everyone. It excites me and drives me to continue my career in development.
